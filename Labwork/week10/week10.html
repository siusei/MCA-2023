Transcription Analysis



Above this section, I displayed both score versions in image and audio formats. From comparing the transcription to the original, there are various inaccurate and missing data in the transcribed output, especially when observing the visual score.

Beginning with the audio clips, when listening and comparing the tracks, the overall structure of the transcribed output is identical to the original track in terms of the general melody, harmony and tempo. However, in analysing the details of the audio output, there are noticeable misinterpretation for the beginning chords, key scale, excess and clashing harmony, missing articulations, level of dynamic, prolonged resting bars at the end, etc. The general listening impression doesn‚Äôt sound as lively and cheerful as the original audio, rather it sounded flat and boring with the errors combined above.

These characteristics are more obvious when viewing from the image file. although the transcribed images illustrated that the automatic polyphonic transcription is able to preserve general properties like the pitches, melody, timings and structure of the original WAV file, it is unable to interpret all music notations in the exact format. For instance, the transcription is unable to capture the accurate key scale, speed, change of time signatures and clefs, shapes of melody, accidentals, articulations, but instead picked up some extremely high/low harmonic notes, as well as excess rest signs and bars in the outcome. Moreover, the misinterpretation of the note-length (using quarter notes as common beat value) and the extra bars extend the score pages from 6 to 10 and the bars from 125 to 306 in comparison to the original score.

Base on these analysis, performing automatic polyphonic transcription based on WAV file is difficult to extract accurate information of the music notations unless adjusting details with human perception considering the complexity of music structure and performed instruments.
